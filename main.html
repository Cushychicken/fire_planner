The table generated here reflects the estimated performance of your taxable, 401k, and Roth accounts, given their current value and your recurring deposit to them. The "Taxable" column shows the anticipated balance of your taxable investment account. The "Income" column shows the amount you could safely withdraw from your taxable balance annually.

Instead of showing the current balance of your 401(k) and Roth accounts, this table instead shows the projected balance of each account at age 80, assuming no further contributions were made beyond today. The final column, "Income @ Age 60", shows additional income generated by 401(k) and Roth accounts once you reach a qualifying age for withdrawing funds from them. (This assumes the same 4% safe withdrawal rate as your taxable accounts.) Note that this figure does not account for taxes paid on 401(k) disbursements, which count as income.

All accounts assume a 7% annual return - the historical average performance of the S&P 500.
