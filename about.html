What's the point of this site?

Lifestyle Finance's goal is to make simple, convenient tools for the FIRE community to plan for financial independence.

Our first tool, a simple FIRE timeline planner, is currently available here.

Why?

It's our opinion that many FIRE-oriented sites today don't have an easy way to visualize all the possible angles of early retirement - especially with tax sheltered accounts like 401(k)s and Roth IRAs.

Ever wondered, "How much income will I make from my 401(k) when I'm eligible for it if I retire now?" Do you ponder what your Roth IRA would grow into if you didn't contribute another dime? If so, we've got the tools for you.

Why does the first table stop at age 59?

Age 59.5 is the point at which U.S. citizens can start drawing on their tax-advantaged accounts without paying a penalty. Since this a tool for people focused on early retirement, and it'd be a shame to just throw money away to Uncle Sam, it's assumed that you'd be living a carefree early retirement by age 59.

That's why we wrote our second tool - a table for plotting income from early retirement on to the point at which you are eligible to receive income from your tax-sheltered accounts. You can find that on the same page as our first tool!

Why is the default growth rate 7%?

Historically, the S&P has grown at about 7% annually. This is averaged over a long period of time, however - over the short term, it's possible that the markets can fluctuate pretty wildly. This tool is meant to give a ballpark approximation of when early retirement is possible. Your own circumstances may vary.

Why is the default safe withdrawal rate 4%?

Inflation is a real thing - the trend is that, over time, prices rise, and the value of a dollar shrinks. To compensate for that, as well as adding some safety margin to your anticipated gains, the default assumption is that, when FIRE'd, you only withdraw a maximum of 4% of your account balance annually. Barring cataclysm, that will have you set for life!

Are taxes accounted for in the income figures shown?

No, and that's an important consideration for you in FIRE, and a real limitation of the tool.

We are working on a way to show both pre- and post-tax income from both taxable and tax-advantaged accounts. Stay tuned!
